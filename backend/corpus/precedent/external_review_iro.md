# External Review and IRO Decisions as Persuasive Precedent (General Reference)

General reference on independent external review and how prior overturns are
used as persuasive (not binding) support in an appeal. This is background
guidance; any specific decision must be sourced with its issuing body and
identifier from a trust-allow-listed source before it is cited.

## Independent external review
- Under the Affordable Care Act (Public Health Service Act Section 2719) and
  state law, claimants generally have the right to an independent external
  review by an Independent Review Organization (IRO) after exhausting the
  plan's internal appeals.
- The external reviewer is independent of the plan, and its determination is
  generally binding on the plan for that claim.

## Using prior overturns persuasively
- A prior IRO or state Department of Insurance decision overturning a similar
  denial (same insurer, same treatment, similar clinical facts) is not binding
  on a new claim but is **persuasive**: it shows an independent body found the
  insurer's stated rationale insufficient on comparable facts.
- An effective precedent reference names the issuing body and decision
  identifier, the insurer, the treatment, and why the facts are analogous.

## What not to do
- Do not cite a decision whose existence, issuing body, or outcome cannot be
  verified from a trust-allow-listed source. Unverifiable precedents are a
  hallucination risk and fail the grounding gate.
