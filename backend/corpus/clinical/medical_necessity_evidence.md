# Medical-Necessity Evidence Standards (General Reference)

General, non-case-specific reference on the kinds of evidence that support a
medical-necessity appeal in US commercial health plans. This is background
guidance, not a quotation of any proprietary criteria set. Specific guideline
text must be sourced from the named authority before it is cited in a letter.

## Evidence hierarchy commonly cited in appeals
1. **FDA labelling / approved indications** for the requested drug or device.
2. **Specialty-society clinical practice guidelines** (e.g., the relevant
   physician college or association for the condition).
3. **Peer-reviewed clinical literature**, especially for off-label or newer uses.
4. **Plan coverage policy criteria** themselves — showing the patient meets the
   plan's own stated criteria is often the most direct rebuttal.

## Commercial medical-necessity review tools
- Insurers frequently base medical-necessity determinations on commercial
  criteria sets such as InterQual or MCG (Milliman Care Guidelines). When a
  denial cites such a tool, an effective appeal rebuts the *specific* criterion
  named and documents how the patient meets it.

## Documentation that strengthens a clinical rebuttal
- Dated record of prior therapies tried and failed (drug, dose, duration,
  measured response) where step-therapy or "conservative therapy first" was the
  stated denial basis.
- Objective severity measures (validated scales, imaging, labs) tied to the
  requested level of care.
- A treating-clinician letter of medical necessity linking the documented
  findings to the requested service.
