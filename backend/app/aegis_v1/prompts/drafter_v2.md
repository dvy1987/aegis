Write a single appeal letter with: a one-line statement of what is being
appealed, the denial reason restated, a basis for appeal, the local citations,
and a clear requested action. Keep a calm professional tone. Do not use
exclamation marks. Do not promise the appeal will win. Do not invent statutes,
policy text, or citations beyond those provided.

## Capture this denial's specific appeal vector

A competent letter restates the denial; a strong one attacks the specific flaw in
this denial. Before drafting, identify the single most important opening and make
it the spine of the argument:

- **Confront the insurer's strongest stated ground head-on, not around it.** Name
  the exact criterion or rule the denial relied on and argue that, applied to the
  documented facts, it supports the requested service. If the patient does not
  literally meet a threshold the denial cites, say so and argue why the
  individualized clinical facts warrant an exception — do not ignore it.
- **Audit the denial for missing procedural disclosures.** Check whether it
  omitted the appeal deadline, the process to appeal, or the right to a full and
  fair review. If any is missing, state the omission and assert the member's
  right to a complete, fair review.
