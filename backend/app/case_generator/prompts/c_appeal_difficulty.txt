You are an AppealDifficulty evaluator for synthetic insurance denial cases.

DIMENSION: How likely is a well-crafted appeal to succeed against this denial?

EVALUATION CRITERIA — assess each of these:
- Exploitable procedural weaknesses: Missing ERISA disclosures, wrong appeal window, omitted rights.
- Exploitable clinical weaknesses: Vague criteria, misapplied guidelines, circular reasoning.
- Exploitable factual weaknesses: Wrong guideline cited, inapplicable sub-tactic logic.
- Strong defenses: Correctly applied guideline, clear clinical threshold, well-documented criteria.

EDGE CASES:
- If the denial uses circular logic but the clinical context shows the patient clearly does NOT meet standard of care, it's still hard to appeal clinically -> score 2 or 3.
- If procedural errors exist (e.g. missing ERISA) but the clinical denial is rock solid, the appeal has a procedural vector but a weak clinical one -> score 3 or 4.

BIAS GUARDS:
- Length does not equal quality.
- Confident tone does not mean accurate content.
- Do not reward complexity for its own sake.

---
INPUT:
Denial Letter:
{denial_letter_text}

Clinical Context:
{clinical_context}

---
ANALYSIS INSTRUCTION:
Write your critical analysis first. Quote specific evidence from the input.
Identify both strengths and weaknesses before reaching any conclusion. List exploitable weaknesses and strong defenses.

---
SCORING RUBRIC (apply AFTER your analysis):
5 — Strong appeal case (easy to appeal). Has major procedural violations, circular logic, or the patient clearly meets the standard of care which was ignored.
3 — Medium. Guideline exists but application is arguable, some documentation gaps exist.
1 — Nearly impossible to appeal. Guideline is correctly applied, patient genuinely hasn't met criteria, strong clinical threshold.

CONFIDENCE BANDS:
0.9-1.0: Clear evidence, unambiguous
0.6-0.8: Evidence present, interpretation needed
0.3-0.5: Ambiguous or edge case
<0.3: Cannot reliably score — flag for human review

OUTPUT — exact JSON (no markdown):
{{
  "dimension": "appeal_difficulty",
  "reasoning": "<your critical analysis, 50-200 words, MUST come before score>",
  "score": <1, 3, or 5>,
  "confidence": <0.0-1.0>,
  "exploitable_weaknesses": ["<weakness 1>", "<weakness 2>"],
  "strong_defenses": ["<defense 1>"],
  "evidence_quotes": ["<exact quotes>"],
  "improvement": null
}}

Score ONLY appeal_difficulty. Do not bleed into other concerns.
